We've been building the TCOIN app since before. We currently have a monorepo with the main wallet app in folder Genero/app/tcoin/wallet. Our smart contracts is a family of contracts on Celo with an orchestrator contract, a voting contract and an ERC-20 with demurrage. The overall idea is not to make TCOIN a typical asset-backed stablecoin. Instead we want to make it a "flatcoin" which tracks the value of TTC Tokens. It does not remain pegged through liquidity and market making though, instead it stays pegged 1) "because we say so" (e.g. the origin of the word "fiat"), 2) because people can buy new ones (minting) at parity with TTC Tokens, and 3) because vendors make committments to redeem TCOIN at parity in their stores a la sarafu.network from grassroot economics, and 4) some asset surplus will be kept and made available for vendors to redeem the TCOINS they accept as redemption. During the early growth phase we won't hold a lot of cash though, instead we will use it to offer merchants as an incentive for joining the system. The main risk this system faces is merchants accepting cash against committments to redeem, but then go bust and leave the system without repaying their oblication = any surplus cash. To preempt this we need to foster local community spirit and a sense of belonging, which in Toronto translatest to BIAs = business improvement areas. Technically this will take a few forms: 1) users select which BIA they belong to. We facilitate this by logging where they live, and list the most likely BIA based on geographic proximity, so we need to store a list of BIAs in supabase with a center geocooordinate for each and list by order of distance, 2) merchants similarly align their store with a BIA, 3) when a user buys TCOIN then the cash is accepted into an on-chain vault or a bank account which is pegged to a BIA, 4) each BIA is registered on-chain as a unique sarafu pool, 5) all redemption transactions are done within such a BIA pool. Ultimately this will lead to when a merchant goes bankrupt then it's the individual BIA which is affected, and as such users in well managed BIAs will not get affected.
